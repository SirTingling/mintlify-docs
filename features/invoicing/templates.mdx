Your invoice is often the last impression a client has of a project. A professional-looking invoice reinforces that you're a professional business. A generic one... doesn't hurt, but doesn't help either.

Customize your invoices to match your brand. Add your logo, pick your colors, choose a layout that fits your style.

## Template settings

Go to **Settings → Invoicing → Templates** to customize:

| Element | What you can change |
|---------|---------|
| Logo | Your company logo |
| Colors | Primary and accent colors |
| Font | System, Inter, or custom |
| Layout | Classic, modern, or minimal |
| Footer | Custom footer text |

## Adding your logo

### Upload

1. Go to template settings
2. Click **Upload Logo**
3. Select image file (PNG, JPG, or SVG)
4. Position it: left, center, or right

### What works best

- Recommended size: 300×100 pixels
- Max file size: 2MB
- Transparent background looks more polished

## Colors

### Primary color

Shows up in:
- Invoice header
- Total section
- Payment button

### Accent color

Shows up in:
- Links
- Highlights
- Secondary elements

Enter hex codes or use the color picker. Match your website or brand guidelines.

## Layout options

### Classic

Traditional professional layout:
- Logo top left
- Company info top right
- Clean table for line items
- Summary at bottom right

Good for: professional services, B2B, traditional industries

### Modern

Contemporary design:
- Full-width header
- Larger typography
- Card-style line items
- Prominent total

Good for: creative agencies, tech companies, startups

### Minimal

Simple and clean:
- Less visual clutter
- More whitespace
- Focus on the numbers

Good for: anyone who wants invoices to feel effortless

## Custom fields

Add fields that matter for your business:

1. Go to **Template Settings → Custom Fields**
2. Click **Add Field**
3. Enter field name
4. Choose where it appears (header, footer, line item)
5. Save

Common additions:
- Project name
- PO number
- Contract reference
- Custom terms

## Invoice numbering

Customize how invoice numbers are formatted:

| Format | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Sequential | INV-0001, INV-0002 |
| Year prefix | 2025-0001, 2025-0002 |
| Custom prefix | ACME-0001 |
| Date-based | INV-20250115-001 |

Set in **Settings → Invoicing → Numbering**.

## Footer content

Add text that appears at the bottom of every invoice:

- Payment instructions ("Wire transfers to...")
- Thank you message
- Terms and conditions
- Company registration info

Supports basic formatting (bold, italic, links).

## Multiple templates

Create different templates for different situations:

- **Default** — Standard invoices
- **Retainer** — Monthly retainer billing
- **Project** — Detailed project breakdowns with milestones

### Creating additional templates

1. Go to **Templates**
2. Click **New Template**
3. Configure settings
4. Name it
5. Save

When creating an invoice, pick which template to use.

## Preview before saving

1. Click **Preview**
2. See exactly how the invoice will look
3. Switch between desktop and mobile views
4. Test with sample data

What you see is what your customer sees.

## PDF settings

Configure how PDFs generate:

| Setting | Options |
|---------|---------|
| Paper size | Letter, A4 |
| Margins | Default, narrow, wide |
| Page numbers | On/off |
| Watermark | Draft, copy, or none |
